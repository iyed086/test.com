About Drift Cars

Drift cars are specially designed cars that are built to slide sideways around corners. They are different from regular cars because they have modifications such as a rear-wheel drive setup, a limited-slip differential, and a suspension that is tuned for drifting. Drifting is a popular motorsport that has its roots in Japan, and it has become a global phenomenon in recent years.

Drifting Techniques

Drifting involves a variety of techniques that are used to initiate and maintain a slide around a corner. Some of the basic techniques used in drifting include the clutch kick, handbrake turn, and power over. The clutch kick involves quickly releasing the clutch while applying throttle to spin the rear wheels and initiate a drift. The handbrake turn involves pulling the handbrake lever while turning the steering wheel to induce oversteer. The power over technique involves applying throttle while turning to maintain a slide around a corner.

Drift Events

There are many different drift events that take place all over the world, ranging from grassroots events to professional competitions. Some of the most popular drift events include Formula Drift, D1 Grand Prix, and Drift Matsuri. These events feature some of the best drifters in the world and attract thousands of spectators.

Formula Drift

Formula Drift is a professional drifting series that takes place in North America and Japan. It features some of the best drivers in the world and attracts large crowds at each event.

D1 Grand Prix

D1 Grand Prix is a professional drifting series that takes place in Japan. It is one of the most prestigious drifting series in the world and features some of the best drivers and cars in the sport.

Drift Matsuri

Drift Matsuri is a grassroots drifting event that takes place in the UK. It is a weekend-long event that features drifting competitions, track time, and other activities for fans and drivers alike.
